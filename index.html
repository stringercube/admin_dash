The Great Chicken Wing Toss of '72

In 1972, the Buffalo Bills shocked the NFL by hosting the infamous Chicken Wing Toss at halftime. Fans hurled wings onto the field, causing a delay of game. Legend has it that the opposing team slipped on hot sauce, leading to a fumble that changed the course of the season. The event is still remembered in Buffalo lore as a victory for both football and bar food!

The Mascot Showdown of '85

The 1985 season saw one of the weirdest rivalries: a showdown between NFL mascots. In a fierce battle of costumes, the Chicago Bears' "Staley Da Bear" and the Vikings' "Viktor" engaged in a dance-off that ended with both being ejected. The crowd voted Viktor the winner, but Staley still claimed moral victory by doing the "Super Bowl Shuffle" first.

The Fog Bowl Fiasco

The 1988 playoff game between the Bears and Eagles became legendary for the thick fog that rolled in, making it nearly impossible for players and fans to see anything. Quarterbacks were throwing blind, and announcers guessed at what happened on the field. Despite the weather, the Bears won, leading many to believe they had somehow adapted to "fog football."

The Longest Coin Toss Ever

During the 1998 Thanksgiving game, a coin toss between the Steelers and Lions lasted an absurd 5 minutes when referee Phil Luckett misunderstood Jerome Bettis’ call. The argument over “heads” or “tails” nearly started a new rivalry, and the controversy is still a point of debate in Pittsburgh sports bars today. A classic moment of NFL history.

The Refrigerator's Touchdown Run

In 1985, William "The Refrigerator" Perry, a 335-pound defensive tackle for the Chicago Bears, was used as a running back near the goal line. During a game against the Packers, he plowed into the end zone, creating pandemonium. This iconic moment made "The Fridge" a household name and forever changed how NFL coaches viewed unconventional player roles.

The Snowball Game

In 1985, a game between the New York Giants and the San Francisco 49ers turned into a snowball war between fans. Hundreds of snowballs rained down onto the field, leading to chaos. At one point, the Giants’ head coach was hit by a particularly well-aimed snowball, and players had to dodge projectiles while playing. It was one of the NFL’s coldest and most chaotic games.
